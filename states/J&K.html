About Jammu and Kashmir

Jammu and Kashmir, located in the northernmost part of India, is known for its breathtaking landscapes, pristine lakes, and rich cultural heritage. The region is often referred to as "Paradise on Earth" due to its stunning natural beauty. The union territory has two capitals: Srinagar (summer) and Jammu (winter). Jammu and Kashmir is bordered by Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, and the international borders of Pakistan and China.

Geography

Jammu and Kashmir is characterized by its diverse terrain, ranging from the lush green valleys of Kashmir to the rugged mountains of Ladakh. The region is home to several prominent rivers, including the Jhelum, Chenab, and Indus. The climate varies significantly, with cold, snowy winters in Kashmir and Ladakh, and milder temperatures in Jammu.

Culture

The culture of Jammu and Kashmir is a rich blend of Hindu, Muslim, and Buddhist traditions. The region is known for its exquisite handicrafts, including Pashmina shawls, Kashmiri carpets, and intricate woodwork. Traditional music and dance forms like Rouf and Ladishah are an integral part of the cultural identity. The region's cuisine is also unique, with dishes like Rogan Josh and Kashmiri Pulao being famous worldwide.

Economy

The economy of Jammu and Kashmir is primarily based on agriculture, horticulture, and tourism. The region is renowned for its production of apples, saffron, and dry fruits. Tourism is a major contributor to the economy, with destinations like Srinagar, Gulmarg, and Pahalgam attracting tourists from all over the world. Handicrafts and handlooms also play a significant role in the local economy.

Festivals

Jammu and Kashmir celebrates a variety of festivals that reflect its cultural diversity. Eid and Ramadan are widely celebrated by the Muslim community, while Navratri and Lohri are important festivals for Hindus. The region also celebrates Buddhist festivals like Hemis in Ladakh. These festivals are marked by vibrant celebrations, traditional music, dance, and feasts.

Key Highlights

  • Capital: Srinagar (Summer), Jammu (Winter)
  • Largest City: Srinagar
  • Languages: Urdu (official), Kashmiri, Dogri, Ladakhi
  • Population: Approximately 12.5 million
  • Major Tribes: Gujjar, Bakarwal, Brokpa
  • Popular Tourist Attractions: Dal Lake, Gulmarg, Vaishno Devi, Amarnath Cave, Leh, Pangong Lake
