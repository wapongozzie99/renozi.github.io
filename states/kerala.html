About Kerala

Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is renowned for its picturesque landscapes, serene backwaters, and lush greenery. The stateâ€™s capital is Thiruvananthapuram, while Kochi is its largest city. Known as "God's Own Country," Kerala is bordered by Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and the Arabian Sea.

Geography

Kerala features a diverse landscape that includes coastal plains, rolling hills, and extensive backwaters. The state experiences a tropical climate with high humidity and significant rainfall, particularly during the monsoon season. Major rivers such as the Periyar and the Kabini flow through Kerala, supporting its lush vegetation and agriculture.

Culture

The culture of Kerala is rich and varied, with influences from various traditions and ethnic groups. The state is known for its classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, as well as its traditional music. Festivals such as Onam, Vishu, and Thrissur Pooram are celebrated with great enthusiasm. Kerala's cuisine is famous for its use of coconut and spices, with dishes like Sadya and Appam.

Economy

Kerala's economy is diverse, with significant contributions from agriculture, tourism, and remittances. The state is a major producer of spices, tea, and rubber. Tourism plays a vital role in the economy, with visitors attracted to its backwaters, hill stations, and cultural heritage. Remittances from the Malayali diaspora also contribute significantly to the state's economy.

Festivals

Kerala's festivals are vibrant and reflect its cultural diversity. Onam is the most famous festival, celebrated with elaborate feasts, floral decorations, and traditional dance. Vishu marks the Malayalam New Year and is observed with rituals and festive meals. Thrissur Pooram is a grand temple festival featuring processions, fireworks, and cultural performances.

Key Highlights

  • Capital: Thiruvananthapuram
  • Largest City: Kochi
  • Languages: Malayalam (official), English
  • Population: Approximately 35 million
  • Major Tribes: Various ethnic groups
  • Popular Tourist Attractions: Backwaters of Alleppey, Munnar Hill Station, Wayanad, Fort Kochi, Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary
