About Punjab

Punjab, located in the northwestern part of India, is a state known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and fertile lands. The state capital is Chandigarh, which is shared with Haryana. Punjab is often referred to as the "Land of Five Rivers" due to the rivers Sutlej, Beas, Ravi, Chenab, and Jhelum that flow through the region, contributing to its agricultural prosperity.

Geography

Punjab is characterized by its fertile plains, making it one of the most agriculturally productive regions in India. The state enjoys a subtropical climate with hot summers and cold winters. The land is crisscrossed by rivers, which play a vital role in sustaining the state's agrarian economy.

Culture

Punjab is known for its vibrant and lively culture, which includes traditional music, dance, and festivals. The state is famous for its folk dances like Bhangra and Giddha, and its music, which is an integral part of celebrations. Punjabi cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, with dishes like Makki di Roti and Sarson da Saag being iconic. The people of Punjab are also known for their hospitality and warmth.

Economy

Agriculture is the backbone of Punjab's economy, with the state being a major producer of wheat, rice, sugarcane, and cotton. Punjab is known as the "Granary of India" due to its significant contribution to the country's food grain production. The state also has a strong industrial base, particularly in textiles, manufacturing, and agro-based industries.

Festivals

Punjab celebrates a variety of festivals with great enthusiasm and joy. Lohri and Baisakhi are among the most important, marking the harvest seasons. Other significant festivals include Diwali, Gurpurab, and Holi. These festivals are celebrated with traditional music, dance, and feasting, reflecting the state's rich cultural heritage.

Key Highlights

  • Capital: Chandigarh
  • Largest City: Ludhiana
  • Languages: Punjabi (official), Hindi
  • Population: Approximately 30 million
  • Major Tribes: Jat, Khatri, Arora
  • Popular Tourist Attractions: Golden Temple, Jallianwala Bagh, Wagah Border, Anandpur Sahib, Qila Mubarak, Sukhna Lake
